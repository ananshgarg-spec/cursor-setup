## Post 1: DeepSeek DSpark / DeepSpec Open-Source AI Toolkit

**Date:** July 2, 2026

**Original link:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/juliangoldieseo_ai-deepseek-artificialintelligence-activity-7478349361169604608-Q-vg](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/juliangoldieseo_ai-deepseek-artificialintelligence-activity-7478349361169604608-Q-vg?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAB3K6BEBFFeihBehvABuy_zHDfzmtWj71uc)

**Summary:** Julian Goldie highlights DeepSeek’s new free AI toolkit, **DeepSpec**, and its speed technique **DSpark**. Instead of generating one token at a time, DSpark checks whole word chunks at once, making model responses feel noticeably faster without sacrificing accuracy. The full toolkit is open source on GitHub under the MIT license, so anyone can build their own speed boost — not only for DeepSeek models but also for other open models like Llama, Qwen, and Gemma. The post includes a short video walkthrough of the announcement.

**Main SEO takeaway:** Open-source inference speedups like DeepSpec/DSpark could soon make self-hosted and local AI SEO workflows (content drafts, audits, bulk analysis) faster and cheaper — worth tracking if you run SEO automation outside closed SaaS tools.
