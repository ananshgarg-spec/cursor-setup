## Post 2: The AI Search Report (June 2026)

**Date:** June 17, 2026

**Original link:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nathangotch_the-ai-search-report-june-2026-activity-7473018552040673282-1Yi2](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nathangotch_the-ai-search-report-june-2026-activity-7473018552040673282-1Yi2?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAB3K6BEBFFeihBehvABuy_zHDfzmtWj71uc)

**Summary:** Nathan Gotch shares Rankability's June 2026 AI Search Report with a blunt warning: "SEO isn't dying, but the market is shifting. Change or get left behind." The post reshares Rankability's key finding from 48 months of Google Keyword Planner data (3,751 keywords, Jun 2022–May 2026): interest in "SEO" peaked in mid-2025 and fell ~30% year-over-year by May 2026 — the first sustained downturn ever — while general AI search demand roughly tripled (3.6× since 2022, still accelerating at +85% YoY). The report also notes that ranking and citation have decoupled: overlap between top-10 Google results and AI-cited sources dropped from ~75% to 17–38%. Full report: [https://www.rankability.com/reports/state-of-ai-search/](https://www.rankability.com/reports/state-of-ai-search/)

**Main SEO takeaway:** The optimization economy is reorganizing around AI answers, not clicks — winning Google rankings no longer guarantees AI citations. SEO practitioners need to treat SEO, AEO, and GEO as one converged practice focused on earning mentions inside AI-generated answers, not just chasing blue links.
