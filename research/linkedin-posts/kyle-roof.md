### Summary

Kyle Roof shares insights from analyzing the SEO scores of real websites, emphasizing that many sites have room for improvement even if they already rank well. He explains that evaluating measurable on-page SEO factors—such as content structure, HTML elements, internal linking, and optimization signals—can help identify opportunities to improve performance. The post encourages marketers to use data-driven audits instead of assumptions when optimizing websites.

### Main SEO Takeaway

- Regular SEO audits reveal optimization opportunities that may not be immediately obvious.
- Measure on-page SEO performance using objective metrics rather than guesswork.
- Improving technical and content-related SEO factors can strengthen rankings over time.
- Data-driven optimization leads to more consistent SEO results.

## Post 2

**Date:** December 2025 *(based on the LinkedIn activity timestamp)*

**Original Link:**  
[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-roof-seo_the-page-people-see-is-content-the-page-activity-7475190678185140224---WX](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-roof-seo_the-page-people-see-is-content-the-page-activity-7475190678185140224---WX)

### Summary

Kyle explains the difference between what users see on a webpage and what search engines analyze in the underlying HTML. He highlights that search engines evaluate page structure, metadata, headings, schema, and other source-code elements in addition to visible content. The post reinforces the importance of optimizing both user-facing content and the technical elements that help search engines understand a page.

### Main SEO Takeaway

- Optimize both visible content and the page's HTML structure.
- Use proper headings, metadata, schema markup, and semantic HTML.
- Ensure search engines can accurately interpret page content.
- Technical on-page SEO complements high-quality content and improves search visibility.
